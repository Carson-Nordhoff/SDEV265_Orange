Card Types

This page will explain in depth the different card types

Super-types

  • Land (Permanent): Land represents the magical resources that players can use to cast spells or summon creatures. Players can play one land card per turn. There are various subtypes of land such as plains, islands, mounntains, etcetera.
  • Creature (Permanent): Creatures are the primary way players interact with each other. They can be a summon, beat, or monster. Each creature jhas various power, toughness, and abilities.
  • Artifact (Permanent): Artifacts are any magical object, device, or construct that players use. Artifics provide abilities, effects, or mana.
  • Enchantment (Permanent): Enchantments are spells or effects attached to any permanents, typically a creatyre or land.
  • Planeswalker (Permanent): Planeswalkers are allies that players can summon for aid. They have loyalty abilities and can be attacked by creatures, although players can redirect the damage to themselves. If a Planeswalker's loyalty reaches zero it is considered destroyed.
  • Battle (Permanent): Battle cards must be cast during a players main turn and indicate a battle is taking place. They enter the field with defense counters and must be defeated to be removed.
  • Instant: Instants are spells that can be played at any time, including the opponent's turn. Their effects are immediate when cast and they are then considered destoryed.
  • Sorcery: Sorceries are spells that can only be played during a players main turn and when the stack is empty. They cannot be played at instant speed or as a response.
